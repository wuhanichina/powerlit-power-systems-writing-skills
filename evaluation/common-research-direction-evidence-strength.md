### Evidence Bar
- Main text gives the uncertainty source, sample or moment construction, grid object, voltage or branch-flow target, and aggregation denominator.
- At least two network scales or a scale-plus-variant design are visible; three-phase or multi-period claims need matching systems, not only single-phase proxies.
- Comparison includes Monte Carlo or high-fidelity numerical reference plus at least one analytical or data-driven probabilistic baseline.
- Metrics must cover both moment-level error and distribution or risk-level error when distributional claims are made.
- Sensitivity or ablation is required for mixture number, sample size, covariance structure, linearization order, or update sample count when these drive the claim.
- Solver, tolerance, random seed or initialization, and fallback/switch rules are manuscript-facing when optimization or clustering affects results.

Extra gate for inverse or diagnostic claims:
- The paper must state a non-substitutable use case where the inverse reconstruction is needed rather than a stronger forward probabilistic power-flow pipeline.
- If a forward baseline wins on accuracy or runtime, diagnostic value must be evidenced by identifiability, feasibility, boundary, observability, or missing-measurement results.
- A main-case module that is disabled or replaced by a baseline must be labelled as such in the table, figure caption, and discussion.

Blockers:
- Only reporting case-analysis numbers without derivation, algorithm, baseline protocol, and metric definitions.
- Claiming general superiority when a relevant forward PLF/GMM baseline wins on the primary accuracy and runtime metrics.
- Leaving extreme diagnostic quantities or local CSV paths outside the manuscript-facing reproducibility record.

- `id`: `moment-sdp-certification`
- Scope: Moment mappings, semidefinite relaxation, polynomial power-flow models, feasibility certificates, identifiability diagnostics, and boundary characterization across PLF and OPF papers.
- Project dirs: D:\Research\19PALI_MomentSDP; D:\Research\19PALI_EM_SCA; D:\Research\27POPF_SDP_Letter; D:\Research\27POPF; D:\Research\28MomentPowerFlow

### Evidence Bar
- The paper explains why the theorem, PSD constraint, rank condition, certificate, or diagnostic quantity is needed before proving or using it.
- Mathematical claims include assumptions, dimensions, regularity or Slater-type conditions when certificates depend on SDP duality.
- Numerical evidence reports certificate values, residuals, eigenvalue or rank diagnostics, solver tolerance, and weak/inconclusive handling.
- A boundary or transition case is required when the claim is identifiability, feasibility, or relaxation tightness.
- If the method is slower or less accurate than a baseline, the evidence must show what the certificate or diagnostic changes in decision making.
- The manuscript must distinguish a necessary structure for identifiability from one chosen parameterization or engineering prior.

Blockers:
- Dropping a PSD or certificate section into the paper without explaining its role in the method chain.
- Calling one structured parameterization necessary when the evidence only supports low-dimensional regularization in general.
- Reporting infeasibility or a dual certificate without stating regularity assumptions, solver tolerance, or weak-infeasibility handling.
