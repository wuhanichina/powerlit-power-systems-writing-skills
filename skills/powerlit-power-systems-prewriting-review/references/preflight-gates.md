# Preflight Gates

Prewriting review answers one question: should this work enter formal manuscript writing now?

## Decisions

`GO`:

- The minimum research object is locked and the problem is real inside that object.
- The gap is concrete.
- The technical object is clear.
- The model appears physically and mathematically coherent.
- The evidence package can support the intended claims.
- The target venue is plausible.

`CONDITIONAL GO`:

- The main idea is writable, but claims must be narrowed or specific missing evidence must be added.
- Use this when writing can proceed if the manuscript states a clear boundary.

`NO-GO`:

- The work should not enter writing.
- Use this when better prose would only disguise weak novelty, wrong formulation, or unsupported claims.

`RETARGET`:

- The idea has value, but the requested venue or article type is mismatched.
- Suggest a more suitable venue or form, such as AEPS/CSEE/TSG/TPWRS/Letter.

## Non-Negotiable Gates

Stop before writing if any gate fails:

- No locked minimum research object or small peer problem domain.
- No concrete technical gap.
- No identifiable technical object.
- Model cannot express the claimed engineering need.
- Key variables, constraints, or assumptions are missing.
- Case studies do not test the proposed innovation.
- Main conclusion would require evidence not yet available.

## Evidence Threshold

Do not infer evidence from intention. "Planned", "expected", and "should improve" are not evidence. A writing skill may only state claims supported by derivation, proof, simulation, experiment, field data, or clearly labeled assumptions.
